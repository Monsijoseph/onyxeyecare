About Onyx Eye-Care Foundation

Our Story

Registered with CAC number: 46241, Onyx Eye-Care Foundation was established by Dr. Gemma Oluchi Henry-Ibe to address the critical need for accessible eye care services in rural Nigeria.

In Nigeria, health insurance policies primarily benefit government employees, leaving millions without access to basic eye care. Poverty and lack of awareness lead to preventable blindness that affects quality of life and economic productivity.

Our mission is to make primary eye-care services available to rural communities, providing free eye treatments and surgeries to those who cannot afford them.

Our Mission

To eliminate preventable blindness in rural Nigeria by providing free, high-quality eye care services to those who cannot afford them.

Our Impact

Over 2,000 cataract extractions performed in 15 years across 8 states, with thousands more awaiting treatment.

Our Vision

To establish a world-class eye center providing affordable care and serving as a referral hub for our rural outreach programs.

Our Services

Comprehensive Eye Care

  • Free eye examinations and treatments
  • Cataract surgeries (over 2,000 performed)
  • Pterygium eye surgeries
  • Distribution of prescription glasses
  • Treatment for diabetic eye conditions

Community Programs

  • Eye health education and awareness
  • School eye health programs
  • Free medical outreaches
  • Referrals for specialized care
  • Follow-up care coordination

Our Outreach Programs

Geographic Coverage

We have conducted free eye care outreaches in numerous rural communities across Nigeria including:

  • Anambra State
  • Enugu State
  • Abia State
  • Imo State
  • Cross River State
  • Rivers State
  • Delta State
  • Ebonyi State

Our team travels to remote areas where eye care services are virtually non-existent, setting up temporary clinics to examine and treat patients.

Current Challenges

We currently have many patients on our waiting list with:

  • Hyper-mature bilateral cataracts
  • Advanced pterygium
  • Diabetic retinopathy
  • Other vision-threatening conditions

These patients are blind or going blind but cannot afford surgery. Our free outreach programs are their only hope to regain sight.

Urgent Need: We require more resources to clear our backlog of patients waiting for sight-restoring surgeries.
